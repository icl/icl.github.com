Course Overview

Overview

This course centers around the assignments. Lectures exist to go over material that you will need to know to do a good job on the assignments. The material you find online here in the form of lectures does not conform closely to what happens in class, where the focus will be on examples and critique of sites. Above all this course is about developing 'good taste' in web site design. This means knowing what makes a site work and what weakens its usability. The principles you learn here generalize to interface design more broadly. In today's class we will go over some of the main content areas we will be covering in the class.

How to Submit

  1. Once you've completed your assignment, log onto your class account. From the main page, click on the assignment due date and you will see a "Submit" link in the day window. If today is the due date you will see that little link automatically. Alternatively you can use the SUBMIT label in the right hand drawer of this page. If you have Java Runtime Environment installed - which on most computers is automatically installed you will see a JAVA applet icon telling you that it is loading. It may take 10-15 seconds before that icon appears and another 10-20 seconds before it fully installs.
  2. Once the applet has loaded you will see a directory structure that allows you to drag all your files into the window to upload your files. Using your computers own file manager (called 'window explorer' on PC's and 'Finder' on Macs) find the files and sub directories you want to submit. Be sure that your index.htm or index.html file is at the top level. Drag all these over. You should see them upload. If your files are very large you will see a upload meter showing your progress. Once uploaded all your files and sub-folders should appear in the java directory.
  3. To check that everything was submitted correctly - and to make sure that your links are working - click on the index file. If you get a null it is probably because you have set your browser to block all popups. You need to go to your options or preferences and unblock adrenaline.ucsd.edu. On the PC go to Tools>Options>Content then choose Allowed Sites and type in adrenaline.ucsd.edu. On MACS go to Firefox>Preferences>Content then choose Allowed Sites. Once you have unblocked popups clicking on your index page will open a new browser. But the page you first see may say something else. So you may have to look for the popped up browser since it may be behind another browser.

Remember, every time you re-submit an assignment, the timestamp will change andoverwrite any previous submission time record. The graders use the latest listed timestamp to determine if the assignment was submitted on time so make sure you submit a little earlier in case you have to modify anything.
